# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Industry recruiters and hiring managers first — they arrive skimming, often from a resume link, and decide in seconds whether to dig in. Faculty and research collaborators second — they care about research depth, publications, and the lab work. Both may arrive on a phone.

## Product Purpose

Soumyajyoti Dutta's personal portfolio. It exists to convert a visit into an email: a visitor should instantly learn who he is, what he does, and that he is **open to work** — with zero interaction — and then be charmed into exploring deeper. Success is a recruiter or collaborator reaching out.

## Positioning

A versatile ML engineer who ships real systems — from LLM post-training and agentic pipelines to applied cyberdefense — and whose portfolio is itself a working demo: a drivable, low-poly 3D world you explore by car.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: email him. Secondary: view the CV (PDF).
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "Versatile ML engineer, open to work — and he built a drivable 3D portfolio."
- Belief ladder: (1) he's open to work and easy to reach → (2) he ships serious ML systems, not toy notebooks → (3) he's versatile across LLMs, security, systems, and full-stack → (4) he'd be great to work with; the game itself proves craft and personality.
- Proof on hand: YaraGen (Booz Allen Hamilton–sponsored, USENIX Security 2026 in prep), AutoPYara (ACSAC 2026 submission, open-sourced on PyPI), 1st place in the CSCE 689 ML-Based Cyberdefenses competition, Springer book chapter (2022), proactive research-agent manuscript (2026). Full record in `base_resume.tex`.

## Brand Personality

Adventurous, warm, open. "Come along for the ride" energy: a cute low-poly world and a friendly car invite exploration, while the content underneath stays rigorous and specific. Whimsy in the vehicle, precision in the payload.

## Anti-references

- The generic Tailwind-template portfolio (gray cards, blue links) this site used to be.
- Dark "hacker terminal" aesthetics — the security work should not read as costume.
- AAA-game spectacle: heavy loaders, forced intros, or any experience that gates the summary behind interaction.

## Design Principles

1. **Zero-effort headline.** Who he is, what he does, and "open to work" land before the visitor touches anything.
2. **The portfolio is the proof.** The 3D world is not decoration; it demonstrates the engineering it describes.
3. **Play is optional, never a gate.** Every piece of content is reachable in a plain scrollable view; the drive is the delightful path, not the only path.
4. **Warm adventure over spectacle.** Cute, soft, low-poly charm; no tech flexing for its own sake.
5. **Fast on a phone.** Static hosting, CDN modules, lightweight assets; the world loads politely or steps aside.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The full content exists as a normal scrollable page — usable by screen readers, keyboards, search engines, and hurried recruiters alike. The 3D drive honors `prefers-reduced-motion`, supports keyboard and touch controls, and never traps focus or content behind it.
